Who we are

Somma's Yummies is a High Energy Nutritious Food and Beverage brand, made from 100% local African staples, for Infants and Young children aged 6 months to 5years.

These Nigeria's leading innovative baby food products contain no preservatives, artificial additives or chemical. They are the natural goodness every child requires for growth.

Somma's Yummies was established in line with the Sustainable Development Goals 2 & 3 which aims to "End hunger, Achieve for security, Improve nutrition, Ensure healthy lives, and Promote well-being".

Using the best of Africa's crops, grains, fruits, vegetables and herbs; Somma's Yummies creates organic blends of baby food products for children aged 6 months to 5 years. Somma's Yummies leads the baby food market with its yummy, healthy and superior quality products.

To be the preferred provider of baby food products to mothers across the globe through our innovative products, creative packaging, competitive pricing and distribution strategy, thereby contributing significantly to addressing the very real problem of malnutrition in children.

How it all started...

Mmachukwu Orizu is a Child Nutrition Advisor, Baby Food Entrepreneur and Health Promotion Specialist for Infants and Young Children. She's popularly know as "The Mumtritionist", a name she earned by being not just a Nutritionist but a Mum as well. Her undergraduate degree was in Basic Medical Sciences from University of Benin while she acquired her other professional/certificate degrees in Nutrition from Shaw Academy, Dublin, Ireland. She is the Founder and Managing Director of Mahauty Health Solutions, a Health Promotion company for infants and young children.

In 2013, as a Clinical biochemist working with a hospital laboratory, Mmachukwu was increasingly alarmed by the number of patients who came to the hospital presenting with conditions easily resolved through better nutrition - the right food. "I saw several cases that couldn't be managed which eventually led to death. Each preventable death brought tears to my heart but none broke my heart like times I saw mothers lose their kids, vice versa. It got to a point where I lost fulfilment in my job and needed to search for a career option that would avert nutritionally-preventable diseases, especially among women and children. I resigned from my job".

The answer revealed itself when she had her daughter two years later. Mmachukwu learnt that at 6months, right after exclusive breast feeding, Nigerian babies were fed with complementary foods that are pre-packaged, full of artificial additives and nutritionally-imbalanced.
